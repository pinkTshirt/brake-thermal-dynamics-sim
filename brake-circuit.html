🧠 Hydraulics Knowledge Center

Hold the pedal above. Every stage — pedal leverage, hydraulic multiplication, the proportioning valve, and caliper clamp force — is calculated live from your inputs.

1. Pedal Leverage

The pedal is a lever. Pivot geometry multiplies foot force before it reaches the master cylinder pushrod (pedal ratio, typically 4:1–6:1 in race applications):

Pushrod Force (N) = Pedal Force (N) × Pedal Ratio

2. Pascal's Law & Hydraulic Pressure

The pushrod compresses fluid in the MC bore. Pressure (force per area) is transmitted equally to every line and caliper piston connected to that circuit — brake fluid is incompressible:

Pressure (Pa) = Pushrod Force (N) / Bore Area (m²), where Bore Area = π × (d/2)²

3. Why the Master Cylinder Is Split in Two

A tandem (dual-circuit) master cylinder has two pistons in series, each feeding an independent circuit. If one circuit leaks, the other still builds pressure — partial braking rather than total failure. This diagram routes the secondary piston direct to the fronts; the primary piston goes through the proportioning valve to the rears.

4. The Proportioning Valve

Under hard braking, weight transfers forward. Rear tires carry less load, so they can supply less grip. Without a valve, equal pressure would lock the rears first. The valve limits how far rear pressure can rise past a knee point:

Rear Pressure = MC Pressure, if MC ≤ Knee Rear Pressure = Knee + (MC − Knee) × Slope, if MC > Knee

5. Caliper Clamp Force & Brake Torque

Line pressure acts on caliper piston faces, clamping the pad onto the rotor. That force at the effective friction radius — on both pad faces — generates stopping torque, the same value that feeds the thermal model on the main page:

Clamp Force (N) = Line Pressure (Pa) × Piston Area (m²) × Piston Count Brake Torque (N·m) = 2 × Pad μ × Clamp Force × Effective Rotor Radius (m)

The Force Gain gauge shows how a few hundred newtons at the pedal turns into multi-kilonewton clamp force at each caliper — pedal leverage and Pascal's Law doing the work.
