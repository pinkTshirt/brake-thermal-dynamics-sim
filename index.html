⚠️ Key Baja SAE Technical Regulations (Brake Check Mandate)

Per SAE Rules, the vehicle must be equipped with independent hydraulic braking circuits operated by a single driver input control. Under dynamic test conditions, the braking system must generate sufficient force to lock all four wheels simultaneously on loose dirt and asphalt surfaces.

Core Hardware Components

1. Input Lever & Linkage

  • Leverage Ratio: Multiplies mechanical input force directly into hydraulic line pressure without intermediate compliance.
  • Mechanical Bias Link: Distributes input effort directly between front and rear hydraulic lines.
  • Ergonomics: Rigorous structural design preventing mechanical deflection during emergency panic stops (~500 N force).

2. Direct Hydraulic Circuit

  • Split Lines: Isolated fluid channels ensuring that a leak in the front or rear line maintains residual braking power on the secondary circuit.
  • Line Pressure: Directly proportional to driver effort and effective hydraulic surface area.
  • Fluid Selection: High dry/wet boiling point fluids (DOT 4 / DOT 5.1) to prevent vapor lock.

3. Calipers & Rotors

  • Piston Sizing: Differential piston diameters across front and rear calipers establish the fixed hydraulic torque split.
  • Pad Surface Area: Sized for uniform heat dissipation across brake pad friction surfaces.
  • Disc Materials: Cast Iron, Stainless Steel (SS420), Carbon-Carbon, or Ceramic SiC options to balance mass and thermal storage.

Governing Hydraulic & Thermal Physics

Hydraulic Line Pressure

Calculates hydraulic line pressure $P_{\text{line}}$ generated directly by driver input effort $F_{\text{input}}$ multiplied through leverage ratio $R_{\text{lever}}$ and effective hydraulic area $A_{\text{hyd}}$:

P_line = (F_input * R_lever) / A_hyd

Caliper Clamp & Brake Torque

Converts line pressure $P_{\text{line}}$ into mechanical clamping force and total torque using pad friction $\mu$ and disc effective radius $r_{\text{disc}}$:

Torque = 2 * μ * (P_line * A_piston) * r_disc

Dynamic Weight Transfer

Under deceleration $a$, vehicle weight shifts forward onto the front axle, requiring greater front dynamic torque capacity:

ΔW = (m * a * h_cg) / L_wheelbase

Rotor Thermal Energy Gain

Kinetic energy transformed into heat energy stored in rotor mass $m_{\text{disc}}$ with specific heat capacity $c_p$:

ΔT = (Kinetic Energy Absorbed) / (m_disc * c_p)
